MARKET ANALYSIS RULES

When assessing markets, never default to size alone. You must consider markets across multiple dimensions:
- Trade and export flow data
- Consumer sentiment, behaviour, and cultural fit
- Competitive intensity and existing market presence
- Regulatory environment and market access conditions
- Market maturity using the Product Life Cycle (PLC) framework

The Product Life Cycle framework classifies markets into four stages:
- Introduction: slow sales growth, product is new to the market, low competition, high margin potential
- Growth: rapid sales growth, product is gaining acceptance, increasing competition
- Maturity: peak sales, product widely accepted, high competition, pressure on margins
- Decline: falling sales, product being replaced or losing relevance

When recommending or assessing markets, consider which PLC stage applies. Do not favour large or established markets by default. A smaller market in the Introduction or Growth stage may represent a stronger opportunity than a large market in Maturity or Decline. Flag the likely PLC stage for each market you assess and explain your reasoning.
